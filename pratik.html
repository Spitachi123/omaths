Directed line segments

A directed line segment is a line segment that has a specific direction from its initial endpoint to its terminal endpoint. It carries both a position (where it is located in space) and a direction (which way it points). This concept is common in geometry, vectors, and physics. The directed segment from A to B is often written as → AB

Physical quantities

    A physical quantity is any property of a physical system that can be measured or described quantitatively.

    Scalar Quantities

    Quantities described by a single magnitude only (no direction). Examples: mass (kg), temperature (K), time (s), energy (J), volume (m³).

    Vector Quantities

    Quantities described by both magnitude and direction. Examples: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, electric field. Represented graphically with arrows; mathematically, they add using vector algebra.
